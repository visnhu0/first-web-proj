Marriage and Wedding: Two Sides of the Same Coin

                                                         "Marriage and wedding are two words that are often used interchangeably, but they have different meanings and implications. A wedding is a one-time event that marks the official beginning of a marriage, while a marriage is a long-term relationship that lasts beyond the wedding day. A wedding is a celebration of love and a promise of commitment, while a marriage is a challenge of love and a fulfillment of commitment."
                                                             "A wedding is a moment of joy and a milestone of life, while a marriage is a journey of joy and a series of milestones. A wedding is a reflection of the couple's personality and relationship, while a marriage is an evolution of the couple's personality and relationship. A wedding is an expression of love and a declaration of commitment, while a marriage is a demonstration of love and a realization of commitment. A wedding and a marriage are two sides of the same coin. They are both important and valuable, but they are not the same. A wedding is the beginning of a marriage, but it is not the end. A marriage is the continuation of a wedding, but it is not the only thing. A wedding and a marriage are both part of the same story, but they are not the whole story."
